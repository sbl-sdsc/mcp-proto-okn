The NASA GES DISC knowledge graph describes NASA Earth science satellite datasets and the research that uses them. It models datasets and the data centers that distribute them, primarily NASA Distributed Active Archive Centers (DAACs), together with the platforms (satellites and spacecraft) and instruments that collect the data, the projects and missions the data belong to, and the science keywords that categorize each dataset by domain. Datasets carry metadata such as CMR identifiers, DOIs, DAAC assignments, temporal coverage, and abstracts. A bibliometric layer links publications to the datasets they use or cite, and connects publications to their authors and the authors to their institutions, with ORCID, ROR, OpenAlex, and DOI identifiers. Reified statements attach edge attributes such as author position and dataset co-usage weight. The graph answers questions about which center distributes a dataset, which platform and instrument produced it, which publications and authors use a dataset, dataset citation and co-usage networks, and author and institution affiliations.
