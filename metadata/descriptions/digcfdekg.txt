The CFDE REVEAL Knowledge Graph (digcfdekg) is a statistically inferred genomic evidence graph that connects human genes to traits, diseases, and phenotype endpoints within the NIH Common Fund Data Ecosystem (CFDE). Datasets from CFDE data-generation programs are represented as gene sets, which are linked to roughly 7,000 human traits using two Bayesian methods: PIGEAN (Priors Inferred from GEne ANnotations), which jointly infers which of about 20,000 genes are trait-relevant and which gene sets predict that relevance, and EAGGL (Enrichment Analysis, Gene Grouping, and LLMs), which learns around 4,000 latent factors that model shared disease mechanisms. The graph contains genes, gene sets, traits, and latent factors connected by gene-to-trait, gene-to-factor, gene-set-to-trait, and related edges, each reified as an association annotated with a probability weight, score type, and generating method. Genes use NCBI Gene identifiers and traits use Orphanet, EFO, MONDO, and HPO. It supports questions about gene-trait associations, latent disease mechanisms, and the gene sets that predict them.
