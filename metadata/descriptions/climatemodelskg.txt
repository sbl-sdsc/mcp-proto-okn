The Climate Models Knowledge Graph integrates structured information about climate models, experiments, and the scientific literature that describes them, supporting climate science evaluation and model development. Built for climate researchers, model developers, and policy analysts, it contains approximately 1.7 million triples describing about 112,500 entities across 51 classes. The graph centers on climate model documentation, linking 394 Sources (GCMs and RCMs) to 481 Experiments conducted by 132 Institutes and producing 3,144 climate Variables measured by 1,490 Instruments. Geographic coverage is extensive, spanning 30,062 Cities, 252 Countries, and 3,893 subdivisions, with entities linked to GeoNames identifiers for geospatial integration. A literature layer connects 2,000 Papers, 10,437 Authors, and 373 publication Venues to methods, results, and evaluation metrics. Its custom ontology structures relationships between models, physical schemes, instruments, variables, metrics, and results. The knowledge graph supports CMIP6-related research and regional climate modeling studies.
